Adaptive Trajectory Control for a Robotic Arm Subject to Varying Load

Robot Controls

Goal:

Implementing an adaptive controller, to handle objects of different or dynamic masses.

Abstract:

The focus of this paper was to implement two methods for an adaptive trajectory tracking control on a robotic arm manipulator such that the robot arm could maintain its desired trajectory while carrying objects of different masses. Two methods were implemented and their performances were compared.
The adaptive controller implemented by the first technique relied on linear parameterization. while the second method was a modified version of adaptive control, that eliminates the need of linear parameterization. It worked on representing the unkown mass in the form of a Function approximation technique (fourier series).
In both methods, the control law had to be updated in a way that it adjusted the gain such that the output did not change with changing parameters of the dynamic model (mass of the object,in this instance). Subsequently, the robot arm was made to follow a given trajectory while also accommodating for the change in mass of the object it was carrying.
Various experiments were performed, with constant masses, masses varying as a function of time etc. As per our results, the second method outperformed the first one in both time and computational complexity.
